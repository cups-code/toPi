# Applications in 2D
## Learning Intentions
- I can apply trigonometry in word problems involving an angle of elevation.
- I can apply trigonometry to problem solve in word problems involving an angle of depression.
## Terminology
- Direct distance refers to the hypotenuse of the triangle.
- The ground is *usually* the adjacent side of the triangle.
- Any verticle height is *usually* the opposite side of the triangle.
## Example
> [!Example] Ex 6C, Q2
> The *height* of a building is **46 m**. The *angle of elevation* from the street corner to the top of the building is **$62^{\circ}$**. What is the *direct distance* from the street corner to the top of the building, to the **nearest metre**?
> - In this example, you would visualise a triangle with the given information. You can see the information [here](https://schoolsnsw-my.sharepoint.com/personal/alexis_kim3_det_nsw_edu_au/_layouts/15/Doc.aspx?sourcedoc=%7B1b95912f-87e8-4c02-9d27-f9d0c0905697%7D&action=view&wd=target%28_Class+Notes%2FCh6+Trigonometry.one%7Cc7a8868f-613c-7e41-b37b-a676f545df9e%2FFor+my+Future+Forgetful+Self+%28FFFS%5C%29%7C5b457a11-6d32-6f42-932e-ad5a10d78eb3%2F%29&wdorigin=703&wdpartid=%7Bfd475bc1-23ab-a643-9fa1-71f245cdc196%7D%7B73%7D&wdsectionfileid=7bd3591e-e491-4cc2-b4e7-897612b47619).
> 	- This scenario involves the sine trigonometric ratio. In this case, you would use $\sin{\theta} = \frac{O}{H}$.
> 	- You can easily substitute the numbers into the ratio.
> - After substituting the given values, you can solve the equation using trigonometry.
> $$\sin{62^{\circ}} = \frac{46m}{D}$$
> $$D = \frac{46}{\sin{62^{\circ}}}$$
