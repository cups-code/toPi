# Scientific Notation
## Learning Intentions
- I can convert from scientific notation to a basic numeral.
- I can convert to scientific notation using significant figures.
- I can use technology to perform calculations in scientific notation.
## Understanding Scientific Notation
> [!Info] Scientific Notation Form (Standard Form)
> $$a \times 10^m$$
> - $a$ is a number from 1-10 ($-10 < a < 10$)
> - and $m$ is an integer.

- To write numbers using scientific notation, place the decimal point after the **first non-zero digit** then multiply by a power of 10.
	- Large numbers are written using positive powers of 10.
	- Small numbers are written using negative powers of 10.

> [!Example] Applying The Form (Positive)
> $$5 \times 10^3 = 5 \times 1000 = 5000$$
> $$3 \times 10^4 = 3 \times 10 000 = 30 000$$
> $$4 \times 10^5 = 4 \times 100 000 = 400 000$$

> [!Example] Applying The Form (Negative)
> $$2 \times 10^{-4} = \frac{2}{10^4} = \frac{2}{10000} = 0.0002$$
> $$9 \times 10^{-3} = \frac{9}{10^3} = \frac{9}{1000} = 0.009$$
> $$8 \times 10^{-2} = \frac{8}{10^2} = \frac{8}{100} = 0.08$$

> [!Question] What is $\frac{a}{10^m}$?
> - Remember, we covered [[L02 - Negative Indices|negative indices]] here.
> - Take for example, $2 \times 10^{-4}$. We know $10^{-4} = \frac{1}{10^4}$. This can be simplified to $2 \times \frac{1}{10^4} = \frac{2}{10^4}$.
## Significant Figures
- Significant figures are non-zero digits.

> [!Example] Rounding Numbers to Significant Figures
> $$763 \approx 800 \space \text{(1 s.f.)}$$
> $$0.0643 \approx 0.06 \space \text{(1 s.f.)}$$
> $$8.2754 \approx 8.3 \space \text{(2 s.f.)}$$
