# Precipitation Reactions
## Definition
- Precipitation reactions are a type of double displacement reactions that create an insoluble solid (also known as a precipitate) from two aqueous solutions.
- The aqueous solutions are the reactants and the precipitate (+ another aqueous solution) are the products.
- However, precipitates do not always form when two aqueous solutions are combined. For example, $KL_{(aq)} + NaCl_{(aq)}$ form nothing.
> [!info] Formula
> The general form of the reaction follows the below.
> $$AB_{(aq)} + CD_{(aq)} \rightarrow AD_{(s)} + CB_{(aq)}$$
## Solubility Rules
- Nitrates ($NO_{3-}$) are soluble
- Group 1 and ammonium ($NH_{4+}$) compounds are soluble
- Chlorides ($Cl-$), bromides ($Br$), and iodides are soluble except with $Ag^+$ and $Pb^{2+}$
- Sulfates
- Hydroxides
