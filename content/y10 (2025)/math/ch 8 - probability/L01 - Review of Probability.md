# Review of Probability (Ex 8A)
*Review of Probability (Consolidating)*

> [!tip] Examination Info
> This lesson **will be included** in the half-yearly examination.

## Learning Intentions
- I can calculate simple theoretical probabilities.
- I can calculate simple experimental probabilities.

## Fundamentals
- Trials
	- e.g. roll of a die
- Sample Space
	- All possible outcomes of a chance experiment as a list.
	- e.g. $S = \{1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6\}$
- Outcome
	- A possible result.
- Event
	- A collection of favourable outcomes (outcomes that are wanted).
	- This is displayed as $P(event)$.
	- e.g. $P(even) = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$
- Equally Likely Outcomes
	- Outcomes that have the same chance of occurring (same probability).
- Language of Probability
	- 0% = impossible
	- 0% < $P$ < 50% = unlikely
	- 50% = even chance
	- 50% < $P$ < 100% = likely
	- 100% = certain
- Theoretical Probability
	- What is expected to happen in theory.
	- e.g. $P(\fbox{rolling  a 3}) = \frac{1}{6}$
- Experimental Probability
	- What is calculated from an experiment (what actually happened).
