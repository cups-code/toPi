# Cosine Rule

> [!Info] Cosine Rule
> $$c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos{(C)}$$
> To use the cosine rule, we need:
> - Two sides and an *included* angle **OR**
> - an angle and three sides

> [!tip] Alternate Cosine Rule
> $$cos{(C)} = \frac{a^2 + b^2 - c^2}{2ab}$$
> - This is used to find an angle instead of a side.
> - This version is an easier way of finding an angle without having to rearrange the formula yourself.
