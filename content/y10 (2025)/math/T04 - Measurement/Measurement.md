# Measurement

## Formulas
## Perimeter
*the continuous line forming the boundary of a closed geometric figure*
### Quadrilaterals
- Add all four sides.
### Polygons
- Triangle: $P = S_1 + S_2 + S_3$ (add all three sides)
	- Equilateral: $P = S_1 \times 3$ (add all three sides)
	- Isosceles: $P = S_1 + 2(S_2)$ (add all three sides)
- Circle (Circumference): $C = 2 \pi r$
- Sector: $P = 2 \pi r \times \frac{\theta ^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}}$
## Area
*the measure of a region's size*
### Quadrilaterals
- Cube: $l^2$
- Rectangle: $l \times w$
- Parallelogram: $A = bh$
- Trapezium: $A = \frac{1}{2}h(a+b)$
### Polygons
- Triangle: $A = \frac{1}{2}bh$
	- (Variant: Trigonometry) :: $A = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin(C)$
- Sector: $A = \pi r^2 \times \frac{\theta ^{\circ}}{360^{\circ}}$
- Circle: $A = \pi r^2$
## Surface Area
*the total measure of the region a surface occupies*
### Prisms
$A = A \triangle \times 2 + 3 \times \square$
### Pyramids
$A = A_{\text{base}} + \text{no. of triangles} \times A_{\text{triangle}}$
### Circular Solids
- Cylinder: $A = 2 \pi r^2 + 2 \pi rh \space (\text{circular base + curved face})$
- Cone: $A = \pi r^2 + \pi r l \space (\text{circular base + curved face (using slant height)})$
- Sphere: $A = 4 \pi r^2$
## Volume
*the amount of space that a substance or object occupies, or that is enclosed within a container, especially when great*
### Prisms
$A = A_{\text{base}} \times h$
### Pyramids
$V = \frac{1}{3}ah$
### Circular Solids
- Cylinder: $V = \pi r^2h$
- Cone: $V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2h$
- Sphere: $V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^3$
