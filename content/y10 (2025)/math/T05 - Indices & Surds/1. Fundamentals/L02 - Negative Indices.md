# Negative Indices
## Learning Intentions
- I can rewrite an expression using positive indices.
- I can rewrite an expression with a negative power in the denominator using positive indices.
- I can simplify expressions and apply index laws to negative indices.
## Understanding Negative Indices
> [!Info] Negative Indices Explanation
> $$5^2 = 25$$
> $$5^1 = 5$$
> $$5^0 = 1$$
> $$5^{-1} = \frac{1}{5}$$
> $$5^{-2} = \frac{1}{25}$$
> We can see that after every power we go down, we divide by 5. If we continue this, we can see that $5^{-1} = 1 \div 5$.
## Negative Indices Rules
> [!Info] Negative Index Formula
> $$a^{-m} = \frac{1}{a^m}$$
> $$a^m = \frac{1}{a^{-m}}$$

> [!Question] Applying This Formula
> We can apply this formula through the following examples.
> $$b^{-4} = \frac{1}{b^4}$$
> $$3x^{-4}y^2 = 3y^2 \times \frac{1}{x^4} = \frac{3y^2}{x^4}$$
> $$\frac{5}{x^{-5}} = 5 \times \frac{1}{x^{-3}} = 5x^3$$
### Bracketed Negative Indices

> [!Info] Bracketed Negative Indices Formula
> $$\left( \frac{a}{b} \right)^{-1} = \frac{b}{a}$$
> $$a^{-1} = \frac{1}{a}$$

> [!Question] Applying This Formula
> $$\left( \frac{2}{3} \right)^{-1} = \frac{1}{\left( \frac{2}{3} \right)}$$
> $$1 \div \frac{2}{3}$$
> $$1 \times \frac{3}{2}$$
> $$\therefore \frac{3}{2}$$

> [!Example] Examples
> 1. $$\left( \frac{y^3}{x^2} \right)$$
>    $$\frac{1}{y^3 \div x^2}$$
>    $$1 \div \frac{y^3}{x^2}$$
>    $$1 \times \frac{x^2}{y^3}$$
>    $$\therefore \frac{x^2}{y^3}$$
> 2. $$(\frac{x^3}{y})^{-3}$$
> 
> > [!success] Method 1
> > $$\frac{1}{\left( \frac{x^3}{y} \right)^3}$$
> > $$1 \div \frac{x^9}{y^3}$$
> > $$1 \times \frac{y^3}{x^9}$$
> > $$\therefore \frac{y^3}{x^9}$$
> 
> > [!success] Method 2
> > $$\left( \frac{x^3}{y} \right)^{-3} = \left( \frac{x^3}{y} \right)^{-1 \times 3}$$
> > $$\left[ \left( \frac{x^3}{y} \right)^{-1} \right]$$
> > $$\left( \frac{y}{x^3} \right)^3$$
> > $$\therefore \frac{y^3}{x^9}$$
> 
> 3. $$\frac{4m^{-2}}{n^3}$$
>    
> > [!Success] Method 1
> >    $$= 4m^{-2}n^3 \div 8m^5n^{-4}$$
> >    $$= \frac{1}{2}m^{-2-5}$$
> >    $$= \frac{1}{2}m^{-7}n^7$$
> >    $$\therefore = \frac{n^7}{2m^7}$$
> 
> > [!Success] Method 2
> > $$\frac{4m^{-2}n^3}{8m^5n^{-4}} = \frac{m^{-7}n^7}{2}$$
> > $$\therefore \frac{n^7}{2m7}$$
